    About Star Raiders

    A 2D arcade-style, horizontally scrolling shoot 'em up developed by a small team of three, where players use the environment as ammunition to battle enemies and progress through increasingly challenging levels. As the lead programmer, I was responsible for the complete mechanical/tech development of the game, including core gameplay mechanics, enemy AI, and some level design. I implemented the player's interaction with the environment, enabling them to strategically use objects as projectiles. The game was developed using GameMaker Studio 2 and its native scripting language, GameMaker Language (GML). One of the key challenges I overcame was making the game's elements work across multiple game types (Endless, Duo, Etc). The game is now being used as a course demonstration piece at South Tyneside College, showcasing its technical features, gameplay design, and collaborative development process.

    What I learnt:

    This was my first major project, marking a shift from tutorial-based work to a collaborative, self-driven development experience. I had the opportunity to work alongside Ryan Kidney, who handled the art and later level designs, and Ben Car, who took on play-testing and sound design. Their contributions were vital in bringing this game to life. Throughout the project, I gained invaluable experience, learning the fundamentals of gameplay programming and how to effectively work within an engine. It also provided me with a deeper understanding of 2D graphics programming, knowledge that I later applied in the creation of the RA-Engine.
