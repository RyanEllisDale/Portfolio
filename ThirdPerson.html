    About Third Person

    This third-person adventure game was my first project in Unreal Engine 5 and Blueprint scripting, completed during my first year of university. A key focus of the project was actor interaction, allowing the player to perform various actions such as picking up keys, activating switches, opening locked doors, interacting with NPCs, and colliding with hazards. While learning the basics, I experimented with movement and physics systems using cubes and gained familiarity with the material and material instance systems by assigning each cube a unique identifier. I also explored rigid body dynamics, applied forces to objects, and created interactive Blueprint actors. The game features five levels, with four focusing on controlling a robot from a third-person perspective.

    What I learnt:

    As my first project in Unreal Engine, I gained valuable experience and learned key beginner concepts such as actors, inheritance, the level editor, and importing materials. I also explored character movement and worked with the material system. Since this was a solo development project, it became a cross-discipline effort, allowing me to develop basic art and design skills while fostering an appreciation for the expertise required in those areas.
