    About Robonslaught

    Robonslaught was developed as a prototype proof of concept in GameMaker Studio 2, inspired by the unique RPG battle mechanics of Undertale. The game explores an asymmetrical multiplayer concept where one player is confined to a small arena while another launches hazards from outside. Originally designed for local multiplayer, it also features single-player modes with AI-controlled opponents. Over a two-month development period, Robonslaught grew from a simple prototype with basic sprites into a fully playable experience, featuring a variety of weapons, custom visuals, and refined gameplay mechanics.

    What I learnt:

    Robonslaught was my next 2D project following Star Raiders, and much of what I learned during that earlier development proved invaluable here. With Robonslaught, I focused on a more structured development cycle, progressing through alpha, beta, and a final vertical slice demo that was actively playtested. This process taught me a lot about development flow, bug fixing, and refining gameplay based on feedback. It was also my first in-depth exploration of local multiplayer design—a style that has since become a staple in my work. During development, I gained valuable experience in building reusable character code, making it easier to scale and iterate on mechanics. I also explored different approaches to AI, creating multiple types of enemies that behaved uniquely to challenge the player in varied ways. Additionally, I deepened my understanding of controller support, learning how to map and manage inputs for multiple devices.
